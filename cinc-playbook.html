Texting and email are supplemental — never a replacement for a call. Use them to stay in the conversation when you can't get a live answer.

WHEN TO TEXT

Text after 2 unanswered dials on the same lead in the same session. Keep it short, curious, and low-pressure — the goal is to get a reply that moves you to a call.

Never leave a voicemail AND send a text at the same time. Pick one.
TEXT TEMPLATE — ATTEMPTED CONTACT (2+ DIALS, NO ANSWER)

"Hey [FIRST NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] with the home search site. Tried calling a couple times — texting in case that's easier. Still looking? Happy to help, no pressure."

TEXT TEMPLATE — NEW LEAD (CAN'T REACH BY PHONE)

"Hi [FIRST NAME], [YOUR NAME] here. Just saw you registered — exciting! I have some great options for what you're looking for. When's a good time to connect for a quick chat?"

TEXT TEMPLATE — TRENDING LEAD (ON SITE / HIGH ACTIVITY)

"Hey [FIRST NAME], [YOUR NAME] here. Noticed you've been active on the site — looks like something caught your eye! Anything specific I can pull together for you?"

EMAIL — WHEN TO USE IT

Email works best for: sending a promised resource (lender list, market report, saved search), following up after a live conversation, or nurturing long-timeline leads (6+ months out). Don't use email as your first outreach on a fresh lead.

All Contacted leads should have a follow-up reminder set — email alone doesn't count as a follow-up.

Step 1  Opening Line

The one sentence that starts every CINC call — memorize it.

"Hey, this is [YOUR NAME] with the home search site. I noticed you were looking at some homes in [FAVORITE CITY], just curious, are you looking to make a move in the next few months or are you just browsing?"

DON'T say their name. DON'T ask "How are you doing". DON'T say you're a real estate agent right away. Avoid yes/no questions.
Step 2  Get the 5 W's

Uncover what matters — Who, What, Where, When, Why.

Who are they? · What do they want in a home? · Where are they looking? · When are they looking to move? · Why are they wanting to move?

"Tell me more about your current situation?" · "What else?" · "Why is that important for you?" · "What would that mean for your family?"

If they aren't opening up — use A/B questions: "3 or 4 bedrooms? Home or condo?"
Step 3  Set the Appointment

Close to an in-person meeting using their own answers.

"Let me ask you this — if I could find you that [WHAT] that would get you [WHY], would you consider moving sooner?"

Example: "If I could find you that 4 bed, 3 bath near the highway that gets you closer to your parent's house, would you consider moving sooner?"

Use everything you learned in the 5 W's. This is how you set the appointment.

Step 1  Get to the Why

Uncover their motivation for selling.

"Hi, this is [AGENT NAME] with the home search site! I saw that you were looking to get the estimated value of your house. Why are you looking to sell?"

OR: "What is prompting your move?" · OR: "Why are you moving?"

Learn their WHY behind needing to sell — uncover challenges and position yourself as their best resource.
Step 2  No Listing Address? (Approach 1)

When their address didn't come through.

"Hi, this is [AGENT NAME] with the home search site. I saw you were looking to get a value of your house, but I couldn't get that information pushed over into my system. I do have multiple buyers in the area looking to purchase right now — what is the address of your house?"

Follow up: "What prompted you to start thinking about selling your house?"

Step 3  "Just curious about value / not ready to sell"

Reframe curiosity into a CMA opportunity.

"That's great — 75% of my clients say the same thing when we first speak. Since you're seeking info on the value of your home, let me tell you why a CMA by a professional realtor is much more valuable than the info you get from a website analysis."

Step 4  Seller Objection: "I want to try selling it myself (FSBO)"

Don't argue — reframe around their priority.

"I totally respect that — a lot of sellers start there. Can I ask: what's most important to you in the process — getting the highest price, the fastest close, or the least hassle?" PAUSE and listen. Then: "The reason I ask is — most FSBO sellers end up netting less after factoring in buyer agent commissions, marketing costs, and negotiation gaps. I'd love to show you what a professional sale looks like for your specific home. No pressure, just data."

Don't argue. Get their WHY first, then reframe around their priority.
Step 5  Seller Objection: "I need to talk to my spouse / partner first"

Get the second person on the next call.

"Absolutely — this is a big decision and it makes total sense to align together. When would be a good time to do a quick 15-minute call with both of you? I can walk through the numbers and answer any questions you both have — no obligation, just information so you can make the best decision together."

Always try to get the second person on the next call — never let it become a one-sided phone tag loop.
Step 6  Circle Prospecting Script

For calling neighbors of listings or recent sales.

"Hi, this is [AGENT NAME] with the home search site. Just wondering — I have ready-to-go buyers (or super hot buyers) in your area. Do you know anyone looking to sell?"

Use this when calling neighbors of a listing or recent sale. Short, low-pressure, referral-framed.

Bad phone numbers happen. Here's the standard workflow so your pipeline stays clean and your dial counts stay accurate.

Step 1  Confirm it's Bad Data

Dial at least twice. If both attempts return a disconnected/invalid signal (not just no answer), proceed. A lead that doesn't answer is NOT bad data — keep dialing per the 6-dial minimum rule.

Step 2  Try a Text First

"Hi [FIRST NAME], [YOUR NAME] here from the home search site. I've been trying to reach you by phone — if there's a better number, reply here and I'll give you a call!"

Sometimes bad-number dials still deliver texts. Worth one attempt.
Step 3  Mark as Invalid & Add a Note

In CINC, add a pinned note: "Phone number invalid as of [DATE] — confirmed disconnected after 2 dial attempts + text." Do not advance the pipeline stage. The lead stays in its current stage.

Step 4  Email Attempt

If an email address is on file, send one short email: "Hi [FIRST NAME] — I've been trying to reach you by phone but it doesn't seem to be connecting. Is there a better number I can use to help with your home search?" Do not spam — one email attempt only.

Step 5  Set a 30-Day Re-check Reminder

Set a reminder to check back in 30 days. Leads sometimes update their info or log back in. If there's still no valid contact info after 30 days, you can deprioritize the lead — but do not delete it.

DO NOT set reminders on New Leads or Attempted Contacts — only on Contacted leads and beyond.
